Google Sheets CRM Case Study

Overview

This project is a lightweight customer relationship management (CRM) system built in Google Sheets for small organizations that need a structured way to track customers and sales pipelines without the learning curve or cost of traditional CRM software. It uses a relational-style data model, automated workflows, and dashboard reporting, all within Google Sheets.

Built for: small teams, nonprofits, and solo operators managing sales in spreadsheets.

Problem

New and small teams often use Excel or Google Sheets to track sales and clients. These spreadsheets are usually created without structure, defined workflows, or links between related data. This often leads to inconsistent or incorrect data entry, making analysis and reporting difficult. While professional CRM tools exist, they often require time and effort to learn that smaller teams may not want to invest.

Goals

  • Enforce correct and consistent data entry
  • Support real sales workflows end-to-end
  • Automate key steps to reduce manual work
  • Keep the system flexible and easy to use inside Google Sheets

Solution

A structured Google Sheets CRM built to address the limitations of unstructured spreadsheets. The system introduces clear data relationships, simple workflow logic, and light automation while remaining accessible to users who are already comfortable working in spreadsheets.

Data Model

The system is organized around three main entities: customers, sales opportunities, and activities. Each customer can have multiple opportunities, and each opportunity can have multiple related activities. A central setup sheet stores reference lists for pipeline stages, activity types, and lead sources. These lists power dropdown menus and data validation rules to keep entries consistent across the system.

Workflow

  • A new customer is added and automatically appears in customer reference lists
  • Sales opportunities are created and linked to that customer
  • Calls, emails, and meetings are logged in the activity timeline
  • Follow-up dates are tracked, and overdue activities are clearly flagged
  • Opportunities move through defined stages until marked as Closed Won or Closed Lost

Technical Highlights

  • Multi-entity, relational-style structure supporting one-to-many relationships
  • Dynamic reference lists using FILTER
  • Protected setup sheets that feed dropdown menus and validations
  • Workflow syncing using XLOOKUP + ARRAYFORMULA
  • Apps Script automations for timestamps and tracking next steps

Results

The CRM enforces consistent data entry and significantly reduces manual cleanup. Follow-up tracking improves accountability by clearly highlighting overdue actions. Clean, structured data enables dashboards for the sales funnel, revenue trends, and win-loss performance. The design also scales smoothly as more customers and opportunities are added without breaking formulas or workflows.
